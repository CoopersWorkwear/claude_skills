# Coopers Golf — Logo Concepts Brief

A new Australian golf apparel & lifestyle brand. Reference brand for tone and
positioning: **Druids Golf** — clean, modern, heritage-flavoured, "premium but
not loud," minimalist monogram identity, post-round clubhouse culture, a palette
of navy / black / white / red with green accents and a metallic sleeve logo.

Coopers Golf is its **own new business** (not the Coopers Workwear identity), so
these concepts are a fresh brand system rather than a sub-brand lockup.

---

## Brand snapshot

- **Name:** Coopers Golf (crest variants may read *Coopers Golf Club* / *Coopers Golf Co.*)
- **Founded line:** EST. 2026
- **Positioning:** premium-yet-accessible golf apparel with a craft/heritage story.
  A *cooper* is a barrel-maker — a built-in heritage-craft angle some marks lean into.
- **Personality:** confident, refined, a little playful on the edges (the Druids
  "loud print" side), grounded in Australian links culture.
- **Inspiration cues to carry from Druids:** minimalist monogram, metallic/brass
  accent, clean grotesque type, club-badge roundels, and one or two bolder/quirky
  emblem ideas so the range isn't all "safe heritage."

## Typography directions

- **Heritage serif** (Trajan / Caslon / engraved feel) — crests & clubhouse marks.
- **Modern grotesque sans** (Helvetica / Neue Haas / Inter feel) — minimal, Druids-like marks.
- **Signature script** — premium personal touch.
- **Condensed athletic sans** — sporty stacked wordmarks.

## Motif kit

Flagstick-in-cup · crossed hickory clubs · dimpled golf ball · tee · laurel wreath ·
heraldic shield · ring-text roundel · barrel / cask (cooper craft) · Southern Cross
stars · eucalyptus sprig · golfer mid-swing · kangaroo mascot · links/coastal horizon · low sun.

## How to get the best AI-logo results

1. Square **1:1** for monograms, badges and emblems.
2. Force a flat brand look — every prompt ends with the **style suffix** below.
3. **Text is the weak point of image models.** For wordmark-heavy concepts, generate
   the *emblem only* and set the wordmark in vector afterwards, or use a text-capable
   generator (Ideogram / Canva). Concepts below are tagged **[emblem]** (text-light,
   safe) or **[wordmark]** (needs a text-capable model or vector cleanup).
4. Generate, pick the strongest 6–8 directions, then iterate — cheaper than perfecting all 25.

**Style suffix (append to every prompt):**
> — flat vector logo, professional brand identity, clean geometric construction, high
> contrast, centered composition, solid background, crisp edges, no gradients, no
> photographic texture, no mockup, no drop shadow.
